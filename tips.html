Decoding the Hunter Grade

How we rank IPOs based on institutional demand and market sentiment.

A

High Alpha

  • Oversubscription > 50x
  • Tech / AI / Green Energy Sectors
  • PE Ratio lower than peers
  • Tier 1 Cornerstone Investors
B

Steady Growth

  • Oversubscription 20x - 50x
  • Industrial / Consumer Goods
  • Fair Valuation (Market Average)
  • Consistent Dividend Policy
C

High Risk

  • Oversubscription < 20x
  • Commodity / Legacy Sectors
  • High PE Ratio (> Industry Avg)
  • High Debt-to-Equity Ratio

The [S] Factor

In Malaysia, Shariah compliance is a massive demand driver.

The Stat: Shariah-compliant IPOs have access to Islamic funds and a wider retail pool. This often leads to 30-50% higher oversubscription compared to non-shariah stocks in the same sector.

Cornerstone Anchors

Look for big names in the Prospectus Summary.

The Safety Signal: If institutions like KWAP, EPF, or AIA are cornerstone investors, the IPO has passed rigorous due diligence. These stocks rarely fall below their IPO price on Day 1.

Price Stabilization

Ever heard of the "Green Shoe"? It's your safety net.

How to check: Open the Prospectus (PDF) and Ctrl+F for "Stabilizing Manager". If you see an IB assigned to this role, they will buy shares to prevent the price from dropping below the IPO price for the first 30 days. This is a massive "Safety Shield" for your capital.

Promoter Lock-up

Founders can't just "dump and run" in Malaysia.

The Rule: Major shareholders are usually locked for 6 months to 3 years. This means they CANNOT sell. If you see a long lock-up period, it means the founders are committed to growing the company value post-listing.

Dividend Policy

Look for the "Dividend Policy" section in the prospectus summary.

Passive Income: An IPO with a 40% - 60% dividend payout ratio is extremely attractive for funds. This creates consistent buying demand every quarter, which helps support the share price even during market downturns.

The 9:00 AM Battle (TOP)

Between 8:30 AM and 9:00 AM, you will see the Theoretical Opening Price (TOP). This is the price where the most orders match.

The "Last Minute" Trap: The TOP at 8:45 AM is usually fake. Watch the 8:58 AM - 8:59 AM window. If the TOP is significantly higher than our "Est. Open" on the dashboard, the hype is real. If the TOP starts dropping in the last 30 seconds, big sellers are entering—prepare to sell quickly!

The 50/50 Rule

Fear of missing out vs. greed is the biggest enemy on listing day.

The Strategy: Sell 50% of your shares within the first 15 minutes to lock in a "Free Trade." Let the remaining 50% run until the end of the day or until it hits the Analyst Average TP.

No MITI Scarcity

IPOs like SkyeChip are special because they have no MITI tranche.

Why it matters: Normal IPOs have 12.5% supply from MITI. "No MITI" means 100% of the institutional demand must fight for fewer shares. This usually leads to a sustained rally rather than a "dump and pump."

Broker Heat Check

Watch for "Fully Subscribed" reports from brokers like M+ Online 12-24h before closing.

The FOMO Signal: If a broker is "fully sub" before the official close, it's a massive indicator of retail frenzy. Expect the final Oversubscription (OS) to be 2-3x higher than initial estimates.

Common Hunter Mistakes

Ignoring the IB

Certain Investment Banks (like M&A Securities) have a track record of higher Day 1 pops. Don't ignore the pedigree of the underwriter.

Application Late

Don't wait until 4:55 PM on the closing date. System traffic can lag and you might miss the boat. Aim for 24 hours before closing.

Market Order at Open

Using "Market Orders" at 9:00 AM can be dangerous if the bid/ask spread is wide. Use "Limit Orders" to control your exit price.
